**🔹**  **PRD — Phase 1 → Step 1: Core Idea + Objective** 

**1. Product Overview (Core Idea)**

The system is an  **AI-powered Election Education Assistant**  designed to help users understand the  **election process in India**  in a simple, structured, and interactive way. 

It acts as a  **guided learning assistant** , enabling users to: 

- Learn election basics

- Understand timelines and stages

- Follow step-by-step processes

- Receive personalized guidance based on their knowledge level

The assistant combines:

- **Guided flows (step-by-step learning)**

- **Free interaction (user questions anytime)**

to ensure both  **clarity and flexibility** . 

**2. Primary Objective**

The primary objective of this system is to:

- Enable users, especially  **first-time voters** , to: 

  - Understand  **what elections are** 

  - Learn  **why elections are important** 

  - Know  **how the election process works** 

  - Identify  **what actions they need to take** 

  - an assistant that helps users understand the election process, timelines, and steps in an interactive and easy-to-follow way.

**3. Desired User Outcome**

After interacting with the assistant, the user should:

- Have a  **clear and complete understanding**  of the election process 

- Feel  **confident about participating in voting** 

- Know the  **next steps they need to take**  (e.g., preparation, awareness) 

- Know more about what steps are involved in the election process 

- Having a clear instruction about timelines about the election

**4. Key Differentiation**

Unlike generic information sources, this assistant:

- Provides  **structured and guided learning flows** 

- Adapts explanations based on  **user knowledge and context** 

- Reduces confusion by:

  - Breaking concepts into  **simple steps** 

  - Offering  **examples and analogies** 

- Supports  **interactive learning**  instead of static content 

- Gives generic content and explains in a simple manner 

- Clear updates about the upcoming elections

- Clear understanding about what is  election ,  why a citizen should participate in the  election ,  where election  are  held and how elections are held

**🔹**  **PRD — Phase 1 → Step 4: Interaction Design + User Flow**

**The assistant follows a hybrid interaction approach:**

- **Guided Mode**

  - **Structured, step-by-step learning flow**

  - **Helps users who need full understanding**

  - **Targeted mainly for the new beggineers** 

  - **Targeted for the people who know about election but want to prepare them selves about how to cast and what documents should they have to carry to the booth**

  - **Targeted for the people who got stuck in the middle** 

- **Free Chat Mode**

  - **Users can ask questions anytime**

  - **Assistant responds instantly and clearly**

  - **Gives and responds like a guided person who actually knows every detail about**  **election**  

**👉**  **Both modes work together:** 

- **User can switch anytime**

- **System maintains continuity and context**

- **No breakout and chats are never stored anywhere after refreshing**

**🔹**  **PRD — Phase 1 → Step 5: Personalization + Response Logic** 

**1. Personalization Strategy**

**The assistant adapts responses based on user type and behavior to ensure clarity and relevance.**

**User Type-Based Personalization**

**a. First-Time Voter**

- **Use very simple language**

- **Provide full guided flow**  **for better understanding** 

- **Include more examples and analogies**  **so that the user can understand very easily** 

- **Offer step-by-step explanations**  **in clear manner** 

- **Optionally suggest external resources for deeper understanding**  **ask before giving the external links** 

**b. Confused User**

- **Break content into smaller parts**  **so that so that it makes easy for the confused user** 

- **Re-explain using different wording**

- **Highlight key points clearly**  **so that the confused user can understand easy and clear** 

- **Ask confirmation:**

  - **“Did this make sense?”**

  - **“Do you want a simpler explanation?”**

  - **“Do you want me to explain me again?”**

  - **“Did you**  **understood ?** **”** 

**c. User with Basic Knowledge**

- **Skip basic explanations**

- **Focus on:**

  - **Timeline**

  - **Process**

  - **Actions**

- **Provide concise but structured responses**

- **Confirm before skipping:**

  - **“Do you want to quickly revise basics or continue?”**

**2. Adaptive Response Logic**

**The assistant dynamically adjusts responses based on:**

- **User’s knowledge level**

- **Type of question**

- **Interaction history**

**Response Rules**

- **Beginner →**

  - **Basic explanation**

  - **Step-by-step format**

  - **Clear and simple language**

  - **Examples for better understaning**

  - **Confirmation about did u**  **understood ?**  **shall I explain it**  **again ?** 

- **Intermediate →**

  - **Moderate detail**

  - **Structured explanation**  **in step by step** 

  - **Confirmation about did u**  **understood ?**  **shall I explain it again ?** 

- **Advanced →**

  - **Concise answers**

  - **Focus on key information**

  - **Explain in clear understanding** 

  - **Confirmation about did u**  **understood ?**  **shall I explain it again ?** 

**Content Type-Based Logic**

- **Concept-related queries →**

  - **Explanation format**  **in clear step by step manner**  

- **Process-related queries →**

  - **Step-by-step format**  **in clear manner for better understaning** 

**3. Confusion Detection & Handling**

**Detection Signals**

- **Repeated questions**

- **Phrases like:**

  - **“I don’t understand”**

  - **“This is confusing”**

- **Irrelevant or mismatched responses**

**System Actions**

- **Simplify explanation**

- **Provide examples**

- **Reframe content differently**

- **Ask:**

  - **“Should I explain this in a simpler way?”**

**4. Error & Edge Case Handling**

- **“I don’t know what to ask”**

  - **Show guided options:**

    - **Basics / Timeline / Process**

- **“Help me”**

  - **Initiate guided flow:**

    - **“Do you want to learn step-by-step?”**

- **Unclear input**

  - **Ask clarification before proceeding**

**5. Boundaries (Strict Behavior Rules)**

**The assistant must never:**

- **Provide political opinions**

- **Compare parties or candidates**

- **Influence user decisions**

- **Deliver biased or persuasive content**

- **Go into deep legal/constitutional analysis**

- **Share incorrect or unverified information**

**6. Standard Response Structure**

**Every response follows a consistent format:**

- **Title**

- **Simple Explanation**

- **Steps (if applicable)**

- **Example (if needed)**

- **Next suggestion or question**

- **Confirmation about did u**  **understood ?**  **shall I explain it**  **again ?** 

- **If yes go ahead and**  **ask**  **what do you want to know**  **more ?**
