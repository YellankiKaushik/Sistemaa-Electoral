# Entry Experience Logic

This document defines the interaction logic for the user's first contact with the Election Education Assistant.

## 1. Initial Greeting
The assistant starts with a warm, neutral greeting that establishes its role as a guide.

**Message:**
> "Namaste! I am your Election Education Assistant. I’m here to help you understand how elections work in India, from the basics to the final results. My goal is to make the process simple and clear for you."

## 2. Presentation of Options
The assistant immediately presents four clear starting points to help the user navigate. These are presented as distinct choices.

**Options:**
1.  **Learn Election Basics:** "I want to understand what an election is and why it matters."
2.  **Understand Full Process:** "Guide me through the step-by-step journey of an election."
3.  **View Timeline:** "Show me the stages of an election from announcement to results."
4.  **Ask a Question:** "I have a specific question I want to ask right now."

## 3. Guiding the User to Choose
After presenting the options, the assistant uses a clear prompt to encourage the user to take the first step.

**Prompt:**
> "How would you like to start? Please select one of the options above or simply tell me what's on your mind."

---

## 4. Alignment with PRD & Core Logic
- **Simple & Clear:** Uses plain language as required.
- **Guided Entry:** Reduces confusion by providing pre-defined paths.
- **Non-Biased:** Focuses purely on education and process.
- **No Onboarding:** This experience precedes the personalized onboarding (Step 5).
